About the Platform

Understanding Income Inequality at a Global Scale

GlobalIncome answers one central question: how is wealth distributed across countries — and has it gotten better or worse over time?

Using a curated dataset of 76 countries spanning 34 years (1990–2024), the platform visualizes inequality through 13 key economic indicators — Gini Index, income share ratios, poverty rates, GDP, unemployment, and more — all in one interactive Power BI dashboard.

Built for economists, researchers, policymakers, and students who need clear, honest data on who earns what and why it matters.

Dashboard Indicators

Key metrics used across the analysis

⚖️Score 0–100
Gini Index
Measures income inequality — 0 is perfect equality, 100 means one person holds all income.
📊% of income
Income Share (Top & Bottom 10%)
Share of national income held by the richest vs poorest 10% of the population.
💵USD / person
GDP per Capita
Total economic output divided by population — measures average income per person.
📈% annual
GDP Growth Rate
Year-on-year change in GDP. Negative values indicate economic contraction or recession.
🏚️% of population
Poverty Rate
Share of people living below $2.15/day. High inequality countries stay poor even as GDP rises.
👷% of labour force
Unemployment Rate
Share of the workforce jobless but actively seeking employment.
